The Union

The Union
Founded: 1935 A.U.
Founders: Heroes of the Union
Territory: Modern Ohsa
Government: Republic (1935-1970)
Theocracy (1970-present)
Philosophy: Unity of magic and technology

The Union is the government and philosophical movement established in 1935 A.U. by the Heroes of the Union following the end of the Civil Centennial War. The Union's founding principle is that magic and technology are complementary forces that should work together rather than compete.

The Union governed Ohsa directly from 1935-1970 A.U. under the leadership of the Heroes. After their mysterious disappearance in 1970 A.U., the Church of Embrace assumed administrative control while maintaining Union principles and institutions.

Foundation

End of the War

The Union was born from the ashes of the Civil Centennial War. After defeating Tyrant Vaxara in 1935 A.U., the Heroes faced a choice: declare victory for either the Magus or Tekk faction, or forge a new path.

Archmage Aceta Minoph, leader of the Heroes, made a historic speech on the ruins of Vaxhold:

"For a hundred years, we have asked the wrong question. Not magic or technology, but magic and technology. Not Magus or Tekk, but human. Today, we end the war not with victory for one side, but with unity for all. Today, we establish The Union."

Initial Challenges

The early years were difficult. Many on both sides resisted integration. Magus aristocrats refused to accept Tekk engineers as equals. Tekk workers distrusted Magus administrators. Violence occasionally erupted.

The Heroes responded with both firmness and compassion. They enforced integration while respecting cultural differences. They invested heavily in education, teaching both magic and technology to all citizens. Slowly, attitudes began to change.

Philosophy

Core Principles

The Union's philosophy rests on several key ideas:

Complementarity: Magic and technology are not opposites but complementary approaches. Magic provides flexibility and power; technology provides reliability and accessibility.

Accessibility: Both magical and technological education should be available to all citizens, regardless of birth or ability.

Innovation: Progress comes from combining approaches, not choosing between them. The greatest advances come from synthesis.

Unity: Former enemies must become fellow citizens. Magus and Tekk identities should give way to a shared Union identity.

Practical Application

These principles manifested in concrete policies:

  • Universal education in both magic and technology
  • Integration of Magus and Tekk communities
  • Funding for research combining both approaches
  • Legal equality regardless of magical ability
  • Promotion of intermarriage and cultural exchange

Government Structure

Original System (1935-1970 A.U.)

The Union was governed as a republic with the Heroes serving as the High Council. Key features included:

High Council: The Heroes of the Union served as the executive body, making major policy decisions.

Regional Assemblies: Elected bodies representing different territories, with guaranteed representation for both Magus and Tekk communities.

Judicial Courts: Independent judiciary applying Union law equally to all citizens.

Civil Service: Professional bureaucracy recruited from both backgrounds.

Church Administration (1970-Present)

After the Heroes' disappearance, the Church of Embrace assumed control. While the structure changed, Union principles officially remain in force. The Church maintains that it serves as steward until the Heroes return.

Major Achievements

Reconstruction (1935-1945 A.U.)

The Union's first decade focused on rebuilding war-torn territories. Major projects included:

  • Reconstruction of destroyed cities
  • Establishment of Vyrodin as the new capital
  • Creation of transportation networks
  • Agricultural recovery programs

Educational Reform (1940-1950 A.U.)

The Academy of Practical Magic (1940 A.U.) pioneered a new approach to education, teaching both magical theory and technical skills. This model spread across the country, creating a generation comfortable with both approaches.

Technological Breakthroughs

Union-sponsored research produced revolutionary innovations:

  • Mass Arcane Web (1955 A.U.): Information network combining divination magic with Tekk communication technology
  • Mana Batteries: Rechargeable crystals powering modern devices
  • Levitation Platforms: Magical transportation systems
  • Improved Spellarms: Refinements to Glockius's original war-era designs for civilian use

Note: Spellarms were actually invented much earlier (1882-1895 A.U.) by Genius Glockius during the Civil Centennial War, but The Union refined and adapted them for peacetime applications.

Social Progress

The Union achieved significant social advances:

  • Legal equality regardless of magical ability
  • Universal education through age 16
  • Healthcare combining magical healing and medical technology
  • Labor protections for workers
  • Property rights for women

The Heroes

Known Heroes

The Heroes of the Union included warriors, mages, engineers, and diplomats from both factions. The most prominent were:

Archmage Aceta Minoph: Leader of the Heroes and founder of The Union. A powerful mage who pioneered practical magic and created the M.A.W. Her vision shaped modern Ohsa.

General Ostra: Brilliant military strategist who commanded Union forces. Known for tactical innovations combining magical and conventional warfare.

Genius Glockius: Tekk artificer and inventor. Created spellarms and numerous other innovations. Partnered closely with Archmage Minoph.

Other Heroes are commemorated on the Victory Monument in Vyrodin, though detailed records of some have been lost.

The Disappearance (1970 A.U.)

In 1970 A.U., all the Heroes vanished simultaneously. No explanation was given, no bodies were found. Theories abound:

  • They embarked on a secret mission
  • They discovered something requiring their permanent attention
  • They were assassinated by enemies of The Union
  • They ascended to a higher plane of existence
  • They entered suspended animation to return when needed

The Church of Embrace maintains official silence on the matter, stating only that "the Heroes serve The Union still, in ways we cannot see."

Legacy

Lasting Impact

The Union transformed Ohsa from a war-torn land into a prosperous nation. Its philosophy of combining magic and technology became the foundation of modern civilization.

Union principles remain officially in force, though some critics argue the Church has strayed from the original vision. Debates continue about whether the current government truly represents Union ideals.

Cultural Influence

The Union created a new cultural identity. While Magus and Tekk heritage remains important to many, most citizens now identify primarily as "Union" or "Ohsan." Intermarriage is common, and younger generations often have mixed ancestry.

International Model

Other nations have studied The Union's success. Some have attempted similar integration of magical and technological approaches, though none have achieved Ohsa's level of synthesis.

Ongoing Questions

Seventy years after its founding, The Union faces new challenges:

  • Is Church administration consistent with Union principles?
  • Has the integration of magic and technology created new inequalities?
  • What would the Heroes think of modern Ohsa?
  • Will the Heroes ever return?

These questions ensure that The Union remains not just history, but a living philosophy shaping Ohsa's future.
