General Ostra

General Ostra
Born: ~1855 A.U.
Disappeared: 1970 A.U. (age ~115)
Role: Hero of the Union
Class: Fighter
Status: Missing
Known For: Tactical brilliance, military leadership
Legacy: Ostra District, modern military doctrine

General Ostra was one of the Heroes of the Union and is widely regarded as one of the greatest military strategists in history. A brilliant tactician who commanded Union forces during the final years of the Civil Centennial War, Ostra's innovations in combined magical-technological warfare are still studied at military academies today.

She disappeared along with most of the other Heroes in 1970 A.U.

Early Life

Born around 1855 A.U. in Magus territory, Ostra came from a military family. Unlike many Magus commanders who relied purely on magical superiority, Ostra studied both Magus and Tekk military tactics from an early age.

She rose through the Magus military ranks quickly, earning a reputation for winning battles with minimal casualties - unusual in an era when commanders often treated soldiers as expendable. Her extraordinary longevity (living to 115) has led historians to speculate she may have undergone experimental life-extension procedures, though this was never confirmed during her lifetime.

Military Career

Early Commands (1875-1900 A.U.)

Ostra first gained recognition commanding a defensive position during the Battle of Crystal Peak (1891 A.U.). Outnumbered three-to-one by Tekk forces, she held the position for two weeks using innovative defensive tactics that combined magical barriers with physical fortifications.

The Ostra Doctrine

Ostra developed what became known as the "Ostra Doctrine" - military strategy that emphasized:

  • Minimizing friendly casualties
  • Combined magical and technological tactics
  • Defensive positioning and strategic patience
  • Protecting civilian populations
  • Winning through superior strategy rather than brute force

This approach was controversial among Magus leadership, who preferred aggressive magical assaults.

Breaking with the Magus (1920 A.U.)

When ordered to execute a scorched-earth campaign that would have destroyed civilian settlements, Ostra refused. She was court-martialed but escaped before sentencing, taking her most loyal troops with her.

Joining the Heroes

Meeting Minoph (1921 A.U.)

Archmage Minoph sought out Ostra specifically, recognizing that her military expertise would be crucial to ending the war. Ostra joined the Heroes on one condition: that any victory would prioritize peace over conquest.

Training the Forces

Ostra spent years training a new kind of army - one that included both Magus mages and Tekk engineers working together. This was revolutionary at the time and required overcoming deep-seated prejudices on both sides.

The Siege of Vaxhold

Strategic Planning (1934-1935 A.U.)

When Tyrant Vaxara seized Vaxhold, Ostra spent months planning the assault. She studied every detail of the fortress, identified weaknesses, and developed contingency plans for dozens of scenarios.

The Three-Month Siege

Ostra commanded the ground forces during the siege while Minoph handled magical operations. Her strategy was methodical:

  • Week 1-4: Establish siege lines and cut supply routes
  • Week 5-8: Probe defenses and identify weak points
  • Week 9-12: Systematic assault on key positions
  • Final Battle: Coordinated assault allowing Minoph to reach Vaxara

The siege succeeded with remarkably low casualties for such a major battle - a testament to Ostra's planning.

The Union Era

Military Reorganization (1935-1945 A.U.)

After the war, Ostra oversaw the creation of the Union military - a force that combined Magus and Tekk veterans into a unified army. This required not just tactical reorganization but cultural transformation.

Defensive Focus

Ostra insisted The Union maintain a defensive military posture. She established:

  • City Guard forces for law enforcement
  • Border defense units
  • Rapid response teams for emergencies
  • Training programs emphasizing de-escalation

The Academy

Ostra helped establish the Union Military Academy, where her tactics are still taught. She personally taught classes on strategy and ethics in warfare.

Personal Life

Despite her military background, Ostra was known for her love of gardens and peaceful pursuits. The Ostra Gardens in Vyrodin were her personal project - a place of tranquility in the city she helped create.

Disappearance

The Event (1970 A.U.)

Ostra vanished along with most of the other Heroes in 1970 A.U. Her last known activity was a meeting with Archmage Minoph about "matters of strategic importance."

Theories

Given Ostra's strategic mind, some believe she wouldn't have disappeared without good reason. Theories suggest:

  • She identified a threat requiring the Heroes' permanent attention
  • The disappearance was a planned strategic operation
  • She's protecting The Union from an unknown danger

Legacy

Ostra District

The northwestern district of Vyrodin is named in her honor. Known for its parks and peaceful neighborhoods, it reflects Ostra's belief that military strength should serve peace, not conquest.

Military Doctrine

The Ostra Doctrine remains the foundation of Union military strategy. Her emphasis on minimizing casualties and protecting civilians is now standard practice.

Cultural Impact

Ostra represents the ideal of the warrior-philosopher - someone who excels at warfare but prefers peace. Her famous quote is taught to every military cadet:

"The greatest victory is the battle you don't have to fight. The second greatest is the one you win without unnecessary death."

The Gardens

Ostra Gardens remain one of Vyrodin's most popular public spaces. Many visit to remember the general who believed that creating beauty was as important as winning battles.
