Ohsa

Republic of Ohsa
Capital: Vyrodin
Government: Theocratic Republic
Ruling Body: Church of Embrace
Founded: 1935 A.U. (as The Union)
Population: ~28 million (2005 est.)
Area: ~450,000 sq. miles
Official Language: Ohsan Common
Currency: Union Mark

Ohsa, officially the Republic of Ohsa, is a country located on the eastern coast of the continent. With a population of approximately 28 million, it is one of the most technologically and magically advanced nations in the world. The country has been in a state of relative peace since the end of the Civil Centennial War in 1935 A.U.

Ohsa is governed by the Church of Embrace, which assumed administrative control following the disappearance of the Heroes of the Union in 1970 A.U. The capital and largest city is Vyrodin, known as the magical and technological capital of the world.

History

Pre-Union Era

Before 1935 A.U., the territory now known as Ohsa was divided between two warring factions during the Civil Centennial War. The Magus Faction controlled the northern and central regions, while the Tekk Faction held the southern territories. The war lasted one hundred years and devastated the land.

Formation of The Union (1935 A.U.)

In 1935 A.U., the Heroes of the Union, led by Archmage Aceta Minoph, defeated both the Magus Archon and the Tekk Prime-Director, as well as Tyrant Vaxara who had seized power during the chaos. The Heroes established The Union, a government dedicated to combining magical and technological principles.

The name "Ohsa" was adopted from the Old Magus word meaning "harmony" or "balance," reflecting the new nation's philosophy of uniting formerly opposed ideologies.

The Golden Age (1935-1970 A.U.)

Under the leadership of the Heroes, Ohsa experienced unprecedented growth and prosperity. Major achievements included:

  • Reconstruction of war-torn cities
  • Integration of Magus and Tekk populations
  • Development of practical magic education
  • Creation of the Mass Arcane Web (1955 A.U.)
  • Establishment of universal mana access

Church Administration (1970-Present)

The mysterious disappearance of the Heroes in 1970 A.U. created a power vacuum. The Church of Embrace, originally a spiritual organization, assumed administrative control to maintain stability. While controversial, the Church's rule has maintained peace and continued technological advancement.

Geography

Ohsa occupies approximately 450,000 square miles on the eastern coast of the continent. The country features diverse terrain including:

Coastal Regions: The eastern border along the Sapphire Sea features numerous natural harbors and fishing communities. Vyrodin is the largest coastal city.

Central Plains: Fertile agricultural land that feeds much of the nation. This region was heavily contested during the Civil Centennial War.

Northern Highlands: Mountainous region rich in mineral deposits and mana crystals. Former Magus stronghold.

Southern Valleys: Industrial heartland with numerous factories and manufacturing centers. Former Tekk territory.

Major Cities

  • Vyrodin - Capital, population 4.2 million
  • Tekholm - Industrial center, population 2.1 million
  • Crystalspire - Mining and research hub, population 1.8 million
  • Portside - Major trading port, population 1.5 million
  • Greenfield - Agricultural center, population 900,000

Government

Ohsa is officially a republic, though in practice it functions as a theocracy under Church of Embrace administration. The government structure includes:

High Council: Led by the High Prelate, consists of senior Church officials who set national policy.

Regional Governors: Appointed by the High Council to administer provinces. Most are Church deacons.

City Councils: Local governance bodies with limited autonomy. Larger cities like Vyrodin have more independence.

Judicial System: Courts operate under a mix of Union-era law and Church doctrine. Citizens have rights to trial and appeal.

Economy

Ohsa has a mixed economy combining free market principles with Church oversight. Major economic sectors include:

Magical Technology: The country leads the world in mana crystal production, enchanted items, and arcane research. Exports include Communication Scrolls, spellarms, and mana batteries.

Manufacturing: Former Tekk territories maintain strong industrial bases producing everything from household goods to advanced machinery.

Agriculture: The central plains produce grain, vegetables, and livestock for domestic consumption and export.

Information Services: The M.A.W. has created a massive "scroll economy" employing millions in content creation, data management, and digital services.

Trade: Coastal ports facilitate international commerce, making Ohsa a major trading hub.

The currency is the Union Mark, divided into 100 copper pieces. Exchange rate: 1 gold piece = 10 silver pieces = 100 copper pieces.

Demographics

Population: Approximately 28 million (2005 A.U.)

Ethnic Groups:

  • Magus descent: 45%
  • Tekk descent: 42%
  • Mixed heritage: 10%
  • Foreign-born: 3%

Magical Ability:

  • Strong ability (150+ mana): 15%
  • Moderate ability (70-150 mana): 35%
  • Basic ability (30-70 mana): 40%
  • Minimal ability (under 30 mana): 10%

Religion: The Church of Embrace is the dominant faith, though religious freedom is officially protected. Approximately 70% of citizens identify as Church members.

Culture

Language

Ohsan Common is the official language, developed as a merger of Magus Script and Tekk Cant. Most citizens are bilingual, speaking Common and their ancestral language.

Education

Education is compulsory through age 16. The system emphasizes both magical and technical skills. Major universities include the Academy of Practical Magic (Vyrodin), Tekk Institute of Engineering (Tekholm), and Crystal University (Crystalspire).

Arts and Media

The M.A.W. has revolutionized media and entertainment. Popular forms include:

  • Scroll literature (digital books and stories)
  • Meme crystals (shareable magical images)
  • Theatrical performances (both traditional and magically enhanced)
  • Musical compositions (often incorporating mana-based instruments)

Holidays

  • Victory Day (15 Spring): Celebrates the end of the Civil Centennial War
  • Union Day (1 Summer): Commemorates the founding of The Union
  • Web Day (15 Summer): Anniversary of the M.A.W. unveiling
  • Remembrance Day (30 Autumn): Honors those lost in the war
  • Harmony Festival (1 Winter): Celebrates unity between magic and technology
