Genius Glockius

Genius Glockius
Born: 1898 A.U.
Disappeared: 1970 A.U. (age 72)
Role: Hero of the Union
Class: Artificer
Status: Missing
Known For: Inventing spellarms and chords
Legacy: Glockius Institute

Genius Glockius (born Glockius Ferrum, 1898-1970 A.U.) was a legendary Tekk artificer and one of the Heroes of the Union. He is best known for inventing spellarms and the chord ammunition system, revolutionizing personal weaponry by combining Tekk engineering with Magus magic.

Glockius served as the personal artificer to Archmage Aceta Minoph for fifty years, and together they created numerous innovations that define modern Ohsan society. He disappeared along with the other Heroes in 1970 A.U.

Early Life

Birth and Childhood (1898-1915 A.U.)

Born Glockius Ferrum in the industrial district of what would later become Vyrodin, Glockius showed remarkable aptitude for both mechanical engineering and evocation magic from an early age. His father was a metalworker who supplied components to both Magus and Tekk forces, requiring strict neutrality during the war.

Young Glockius spent his childhood in the forge, learning metallurgy and mechanical principles while also displaying natural magical talent. By age 12, he could conjure force bolts strong enough to dent steel plate.

Education (1915-1920 A.U.)

Glockius attended the Vyrodin Academy of Practical Magic, where he was known as an unusual student who obsessed over the intersection of magic and mechanics. His thesis, "Evocation Matrices in Industrial Applications," was initially rejected as "too practical" before being accepted.

After graduation in 1918 A.U., he worked for several artificer shops, gaining a reputation for solving "impossible" problems.

Partnership with Minoph

The Meeting (1920 A.U.)

In 1920 A.U., Glockius received a summons from Archmage Aceta Minoph, who needed a personal artificer. The interview was brief but memorable:

"Can you make the impossible possible?" Minoph asked.
"Only on weekdays," Glockius replied. "On weekends, I make the unthinkable routine."

He was hired immediately, beginning a partnership that would last fifty years and change the world.

Collaborative Work (1920-1970 A.U.)

Over five decades, Glockius and Minoph collaborated on dozens of projects:

  • Arcane communication networks (precursor to the M.A.W.)
  • Self-sustaining barrier systems for city defense
  • Mana-efficient transportation methods
  • The spellarm and chord system
  • Rechargeable mana batteries

Major Inventions

Spellarms and Chords (1942-1955 A.U.)

Glockius's most famous invention began in 1942 A.U. when he examined captured Tekk firearms. He was fascinated by their efficiency but frustrated by their limitations - heavy ammunition, frequent reloading, and mechanical failures.

"Why carry metal when you carry mana?" he famously wrote. "Every mage has an infinite magazine if we can just tap it properly."

The breakthrough came in 1952 A.U. with the invention of chords - iron projectiles materialized from mana and propelled by evocation magic. This eliminated the need for physical ammunition while maintaining the stopping power of traditional firearms.

By 1955 A.U., spellarms entered mass production, revolutionizing personal weaponry.

Other Innovations

  • Improved Mana Crystals: More efficient storage and transfer
  • Self-Repairing Armor: Enchantments that automatically mend damage
  • Arcane Furnace: Reached temperatures previously thought impossible
  • Rechargeable Batteries: Mana crystals that could be recharged rather than disposed

Role in the War

Joining the Heroes (1930s A.U.)

When Archmage Minoph began assembling warriors to oppose Tyrant Vaxara, Glockius joined as the group's artificer. His role was to keep equipment functional, create new tools as needed, and provide technical expertise.

The Siege of Vaxhold (1935 A.U.)

During the final battle, Glockius's inventions proved crucial. His barrier-breaking devices allowed the Heroes to penetrate Vaxara's defenses, and his emergency healing apparatus saved multiple lives during the three-month siege.

The Union Era

Rebuilding (1935-1955 A.U.)

After the war, Glockius focused on peaceful applications of his skills. He helped design Vyrodin's infrastructure, created the mana-powered public transportation system, and established manufacturing standards that are still used today.

The Glockius Institute (1948 A.U.)

In 1948 A.U., Glockius founded the Glockius Institute of Applied Arcana in Vyrodin. The Institute trains artificers in his practical, efficient approach to magic and technology. It remains the premier research facility in Ohsa.

Philosophy

Glockius's personal motto, inscribed above the Institute's entrance, summarizes his philosophy:

"Magic should serve people, not the other way around."

Disappearance

The Event (1970 A.U.)

In 1970 A.U., Glockius disappeared along with Archmage Minoph and the other Heroes (except Grezer the Steelskin). No explanation was given, no bodies were found.

His last known project was research into the theoretical limits of mana manipulation and the nature of the M.A.W. itself. Some speculate this research is connected to the disappearance.

Theories

  • They embarked on a secret mission
  • They discovered something requiring their permanent attention
  • An experiment went wrong
  • They entered suspended animation to return when needed

Legacy

The Glockius Institute

The Institute continues his work, training new generations of artificers. It houses his personal collection of Tekk firearms, his workshop (preserved as a museum), and his extensive notes.

Spellarms Industry

The spellarms industry employs hundreds of thousands and has made Ohsa a leader in magical weaponry. Glockius Arms, the company bearing his name, remains the largest manufacturer.

Cultural Impact

Glockius represents the ideal of the Union - someone who bridged Magus and Tekk philosophies to create something greater than either alone. His practical approach to magic influenced an entire generation.

Personal Life

Despite his fame, Glockius remained private. He never married, claiming his work was his family. Colleagues described him as intense but fair, with a dry sense of humor and a habit of working through the night when inspired.

He was known to carry a prototype spellarm at all times - not for protection, but because "you never know when you'll think of an improvement."
